Resources

Many local communities have a wealth of resources that can support the effort to strengthen families and help youth grow into strong adults. Staying aware of and connecting to local programs is important. Below is a list of additional resources that can be called upon.

  • Tech Resources

    • Child Welfare Product Hub

      A consolidated hub for user-centered products in child welfare serving everyone from youth and children directly, to social workers, biological and foster families, and community partners. Each product is listed along with a POC who you can talk to about procurement, collaboration, and best practice sharing.

      POC: Kurt Heisler
      Acting Director, Office of Data, Analysis, Research & Evaluation
      Administration for Children, Youth and Families, Department of Health and Human Services
      Email: kurt.heisler@acf.hhs.gov

    • Code for America How-Tos

      Best practices and how-to guides on hiring and retaining tech talent, agile procurement, human-centered design, launching a minimum viable product and data informed decision making.

      URL: https://www.codeforamerica.org/how/how-tos
      POC: Dan Hon
      Digital Transformation Consultant, CA Department of Technology
      Email: dan@gravitas.danhon.com

    • Child Welfare Technology Procurement & RFP Writing

      18F and Code For America collaborated with the State of California in 2015 to reimagine their procurement strategy. Working together, they reduced a 1500 page RFP down to 10 pages that requested the most modern and efficient technological methods.

      URL: https://18f.gsa.gov/2016/03/22/helping-california-buy-a-new-child-welfare-system/

  • Youth Agency Resources

    • WIT-Y: Wellbeing Indicator Tool for Youth

      A wellbeing self-assessment tool for youth ages 15-21 years old who are or have been involved in the child welfare system. In addition to helping youth self-assess their wellbeing, it also provides resources that help young people and caring adults work together to set a blueprint, or plan of action, for improving their wellbeing in a set time-frame.

      URL: http://cascw.umn.edu/portfolio-items/well-being-indicator-tool-for-youth-wit-y/
      POC: Amelia Franck Meyer
      Founder and CEO, Alia; Founder and former CEO, Anu (creator of WIT-Y)
      afranckmeyer@anufs.org

    • Foster Care Alumni Network

      Alumni networks can help youth and children currently in the system reach out to peers for advice, mentorship and guidance. Particularly during the aging out process, these networks can be invaluable sources of support for young people who have lost their agency network. This network has a presence in multiple states and counties and helps foster youth and alumni connect over their shared experience.

      URL: http://www.fostercarealumni.org/

    • Your Life Your Voice

      This is a national hotline, toll free, available to kids, teens and young adults at anytime. Please contact us if you’re depressed, contemplating suicide, being physically or sexually abused, on the run, addicted, threatened by gang violence, fighting with a friend or parent, or if you are faced with an overwhelming challenge.

      Phone: 800-448-3000
      URL: http://www.yourlifeyourvoice.org/Pages/home.aspx

    • National Runaway Hotline

      24-hour crisis line. It’s anonymous, confidential and free.

      Phone: 1-800-786-2929 (RUNAWAY)

    • National Dating Abuse Helpline

      The National Dating Abuse Helpline is specifically designed for teens and young adults. The advocates are available 24/7 to answer texts, phone calls and chats. The helpline also offers translation services.

      Phone: 866-331-9474
      Phone: 866-331-8453 TTY

    • National Domestic Violence Hotline

      National Domestic Violence Hotline provides information for people living in all 50 states, Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands. The hotline is open 24/7 and offers services in more than 170 languages.

      Phone: 800-799-SAFE (7233)
      Phone: 800-787-3224 TTY

    • National Sexual Assault Hotline

      The Rape, Abuse & Incest National Network (RAINN) offers secure assistance on Sexual Assault issues 24/7.

      Phone: 800-656-HOPE (4673)

    • National Alliance on Mental Illness

      The NAMI HelpLine can be reached Monday through Friday, 10 am–6 pm, ET. HelpLine staff and volunteers are prepared to answer your questions about all kinds of mental health issues including symptoms of mental illness; treatment options; and local support groups and services.

      Phone: 800-950-NAMI (6264)
      Email: info@nami.org
      URL: https://www.nami.org/Find-Support/NAMIHelpLine#sthash.0serNAp0.dpuf

    • National Suicide Prevention Line

      No matter what problems you are dealing with, we want to help you find a reason to keep living. By calling 800-273-TALK (8255) you’ll be connected to a skilled, trained counselor at a crisis center in your area, anytime 24/7.

      Phone: 800-273-8255

    • Career, Academic, Skill Testing

      Resources, inventories and information about college, career, and other vocational opportunities.

      URL: http://www.yourfreecareertest.com/

    • Alateen

      Chat live with other teens! Young people aged 13 to 18 who have been affected by someone else’s drinking are invited to share experience, strength and hope with other teens from across the U.S. and Canada.

      Phone: 888-425-2666 (888-4AL-ANON)
      URL: http://www.al-anon.alateen.org/ try-an-alateen-chat-meeting

    • National Eating Disorders Association

      Phone: 800-931-2237
      URL: http://NationalEatingDisorders.org

    • Society for Adolescent Health and Medicine

      URL: http://www.adolescenthealth.org/About-SAHM/Directory.aspx

    • Planned Parenthood Federation of America

      Big Brothers Big Sisters makes meaningful, monitored matches between adult volunteers (“Bigs”) and children (“Littles”), ages 6 through 18, in communities across the country.

      URL: http://www.gsanetwork.org/

    • YWCA

      URL: http://ywca.org

    • YMCA

      Millions of children and teens build skills and confidence as they explore new interests and passions through the Y. Leadership and academic enrichment programs—including Achievers Clubs, Youth and Government and college preparation.

      URL: http://www.ymca.net/

    • Taking Charge of Your Health & Wellbeing

      A way to measure and get help understanding your purpose and spirituality.

      URL: www.takingcharge.csh.umn.edu/

    • Center for Spirituality and Healing

      Healing environments-a wealth of information on improving your environmental health.

      URL: http://www.takingcharge.csh.umn.edu/explore-healing-practices/healing-environment/ what-are-healing-gardens

  • Family Strengthening Resources

    • Parent Partnership Programs

      Parent partners are valuable resources to child welfare agencies seeking to better engage their communities, and the families they touch so that they might achieve reunification and permanency faster and with as little trauma as possible. By engaging successful parent alumna of the child welfare system, agencies can provide current involved families with empathetic partners, and build community ties.

      URL: http://lacdcfs.org/PIP/ParentStory.html
      URL: http://www.ifapa.org/pdf_docs/ResourceParentsPartneringWithBirthParents.pdf
      URL: https://www.childwelfare.gov/pubPDFs/CO_ParentPartnerManual.pdf

    • Abuse and Neglect Prevention

      The following paper and video show the rationale behind, and a potential way to prevent neglect and abuse by support struggling families and parents through parent mentorship.

      URL: https://youtu.be/csCJ3guNU3g
      URL: http://www.centerforchildwelfare.org/kb/bppub/engaging-parents-innovative.pdf

  • Agency-Worker Support Resources

    • Self Care and Lifestyle Balance Inventory

      Social worker burnout in child welfare agencies remains a huge challenge for worker wellbeing within child welfare agencies, and a significant obstacle to permanency for foster children and youth. Studies have shown that rates of permanency drop significantly each time a case changes hands between social workers. By using this assessment, you can get an idea of where your agency stands on worker wellbeing, and areas for improvement.

      URL: http://www.headington-institute.org/files/test_self-care-and-lifestyle-inventory_best_76305.pdf
